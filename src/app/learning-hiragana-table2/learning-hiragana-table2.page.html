A
ten-ten
(which literally means "dot-dot") is a sign composed of
2 tiny strokes
added on kana syllabaries to indicate that the consonant of a syllable should be
pronounced differently
To easily remember how the pronunciation changes, say the basic sound but add a
vibration of the larynx
. For example, S becomes Z when you create a vibration in your throat
